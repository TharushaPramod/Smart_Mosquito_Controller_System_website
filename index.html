Project Overview

Why Smart Mosquito Control?

Mosquito-borne diseases like dengue, malaria, and Zika affect millions globally. Our system offers a smarter, data-driven solution.

🎯

Problem Statement

Traditional mosquito control methods are inefficient, environmentally harmful, and lack real-time adaptability. Communities in tropical regions face recurring outbreaks due to outdated reactive approaches.

💡

Our Solution

A smart, sensor-driven system that continuously monitors mosquito activity, automatically deploys countermeasures, and provides live data dashboards for public health authorities.

📊

Expected Impact

Significant reduction in mosquito populations, early warning capabilities for disease outbreak prevention, and an eco-friendly alternative to chemical pesticides.

Research Domain

Domain & Background

A comprehensive look at the research area, gaps identified, and our objectives.

📚

Literature Survey

Extensive review of existing mosquito control technologies, IoT-based pest monitoring systems, and AI-powered classification models. Analysis of 40+ research papers spanning 2015–2024 in vector control and smart agriculture.

🔍

Research Gap

Existing systems lack real-time adaptive response mechanisms. Most solutions are either purely chemical-based or require constant human intervention. No integrated IoT+AI solution tailored for tropical urban environments exists.

❓

Research Problem

How can an automated, AI-enabled IoT system accurately detect, classify, and respond to mosquito activity in real-time to reduce disease transmission risk in urban Sri Lankan environments?

🎯

Research Objectives

1) Develop real-time mosquito detection using computer vision. 2) Design IoT-based smart trap with automated response. 3) Build predictive analytics for outbreak risk scoring. 4) Create a mobile dashboard for monitoring.

⚙️

Methodology

Design Science Research methodology with iterative prototyping. Phases include requirements analysis, system architecture, hardware development, ML model training, field testing, and evaluation with public health stakeholders.

🛠️

Technologies Used

Raspberry Pi 4, TensorFlow Lite, OpenCV, MQTT Protocol, React Native, Node.js, Firebase Realtime DB, Python, AWS IoT Core, and custom PCB sensor arrays for environmental monitoring.
